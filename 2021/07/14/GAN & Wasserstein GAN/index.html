GAN & Wasserstein GAN

2021-07-14

  • A generator G and a discriminator D

    • G’s goal: minimise objective such that D(G(z))D(G(z))D(G(z)) is close to 1 (discriminator is fooled into thinking generated G(z)G(z)G(z) is real)
    • D’s goal: maximise objective such that D(x)D(x)D(x) is close to 1 (real) and D(G(z))D(G(z))D(G(z)) is close to 0 (fake)
    • G and D are both neural networks, G is differentiable
    • Alternate between “Gradient Ascent on D” and “Gradient Descent on G”
    • Optimisation target:

    min⁡Gmax⁡DV(D,G)=Ex∼pdata (x)[log⁡D(x)]+Ez∼pz(z)[log⁡(1−D(G(z)))]\min _{G} \max _{D} V(D, G)=\mathbb{E}_{\boldsymbol{x} \sim p_{\text {data }}(\boldsymbol{x})}[\log D(\boldsymbol{x})]+\mathbb{E}_{\boldsymbol{z} \sim p_{\boldsymbol{z}}(\boldsymbol{z})}[\log (1-D(G(\boldsymbol{z})))] Gmin​Dmax​V(D,G)=Ex∼pdata ​(x)​[logD(x)]+Ez∼pz​(z)​[log(1−D(G(z)))]

    • The following figure shows the process of optimisation. Actually, we are looking for a map between zzz and xxx. zzz could be any distribution, but normally we use normal distribution. The purpose of GAN is to find out a way to transform a distribution from which zzz is sampled to the target (pdatap_{data}pdata​). Ideally, at the last step, the distribution of the generated data (pgp_gpg​) is 100% the same as pdatap_{data}pdata​, D is not able to descriminate the generated data from the real data and hence output 0.5.

  • Backgroud Knowledge

    • KL divergence - to measure the similarity of two distributions. Smaller number means they are closer.
      • discrete

      DKL(P∥Q)=∑iP(i)log⁡P(i)Q(i)=E[log⁡P(i)Q(i)]D_{K L}(P \| Q)=\sum_{i} P(i) \log \frac{P(i)}{Q(i)}=\mathbb{E}[\log\frac{P(i)}{Q(i)}] DKL​(P∥Q)=i∑​P(i)logQ(i)P(i)​=E[logQ(i)P(i)​]

      • continuous

      DKL(P∥Q)=∫−∞∞p(x)log⁡p(x)q(x)dx=E[log⁡p(x)q(x)]D_{K L}(P \| Q)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} d x=\mathbb{E}[\log\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}] DKL​(P∥Q)=∫−∞∞​p(x)logq(x)p(x)​dx=E[logq(x)p(x)​]

      • Note that they can be expressed as expectations.
    • JS divergence - another distribution similarity measurement derived from KL divergence.

      DJS(P∣∣Q)=12KL(p∥m)+12KL(q∥m), where m(x)=12(p(x)+q(x))D_{JS}(P||Q)=\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{KL}(p \| m)+\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{KL}(q \| m),\ where\ m(x)=\frac{1}{2}(p(x)+q(x)) DJS​(P∣∣Q)=21​KL(p∥m)+21​KL(q∥m), where m(x)=21​(p(x)+q(x))

  • Difficulty of GANs

    • The gradient vanishing
      • At the very early phase of training, D is very easy to be confident in detecting G, so D will output almost always 0
      • In GAN, better discriminator leads to worse vanishing gradient in its generator

  • JS divergence

    • JS(Pr∣∣PG)=log2JS(P_r||P_G)=log2JS(Pr​∣∣PG​)=log2 when two distribution has no overlap
    • The probability that the support of pdatap_{data}pdata​ and pgp_gpg​ have almost zero overlap is 1.
      • support(支撑集) - 函数的非零部分子集，比如ReLu函数的支撑集就是 (0,+∞)(0, +\infty)(0,+∞)，一个概率分布的支撑集就是所有概率密度非零部分的集合

  • Wasserstein (Earth-Mover) distance

    W(Pdata,Pg)=inf⁡γ∼Π(Pdata,Pg)E(x,y)∼γ[∣∣x−y∣∣]W(P_{data}, P_{g})=\inf_{\gamma\sim\Pi(P_{data}, P_{g})} \mathbb{E}_{(x, y)\sim\gamma}[||x-y||] W(Pdata​,Pg​)=γ∼Π(Pdata​,Pg​)inf​E(x,y)∼γ​[∣∣x−y∣∣]

    • Π(Pdata,Pg)\Pi(P_{data},P_g)Π(Pdata​,Pg​) 是PdataP_{data}Pdata​和PgP_gPg​组合起来的所有可能的联合分布的集合，即Π(Pdata,Pg)\Pi(P_{data},P_g)Π(Pdata​,Pg​)中每一个分布的边缘分布都是PdataP_{data}Pdata​和PgP_gPg​
    • 直观理解PdataP_{data}Pdata​和PgP_gPg​像两堆土，位置不一样，形状不一样， W(Pdata,Pg)W(P_{data}, P_{g})W(Pdata​,Pg​)代表把土从PdataP_{data}Pdata​挪到PgP_gPg​所需要的最小消耗
    • 和KL散度，JS散度相比，优越性在于，即便两个分布没有重叠，Wasserstein distance仍然能够反映它们的远近

  • Loss Function in WGAN

    • the infimum is highly intractable

    • convert the W-distance to its duality form

      W(Pdata,Pg)=sup⁡∣∣f∣∣L≤1Ex∼Pdata[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg[f(x)]=1Ksup⁡∣∣f∣∣L≤KEx∼Pr[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg[f(x)]\begin{aligned}W(P_{data}, P_{g})&=\sup_{||f||_{L}\le 1}\mathbb{E}_{x\sim P_{data}}[f(x)]-\mathbb{E}_{x\sim P_{g}}[f(x)] \\&=\frac{1}{K}\sup_{||f||_{L}\le K}\mathbb{E}_{x\sim P_{r}}[f(x)]-\mathbb{E}_{x\sim P_{g}}[f(x)]\end{aligned} W(Pdata​,Pg​)​=∣∣f∣∣L​≤1sup​Ex∼Pdata​​[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg​​[f(x)]=K1​∣∣f∣∣L​≤Ksup​Ex∼Pr​​[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg​​[f(x)]​

    • Lipschitz连续 - 在一个连续函数 fff 上面额外施加了一个限制，要求存在一个常数 K≥0K\ge0K≥0 使得定义域内的任意两个元素 x1x_1x1​ 和 x2x_2x2​ 都满足 ∣f(x1)−f(x2)∣≤K∣x1−x2∣|f(x_{1})-f(x_{2})| \leq K|x_{1}-x_{2}|∣f(x1​)−f(x2​)∣≤K∣x1​−x2​∣, 此时称函数 fff 的Lipschitz常数为 KKK. 简单理解，比如说 fff 的定义域是实数集合，那上面的要求就等价于 fff 的导函数绝对值不超过 KKK。Lipschitz连续条件限制了一个连续函数的最大局部变动幅度。

    • 对偶式的意思就是在要求函数 fff 的Lipschitz常数 ∣∣f∣∣L||f||_L∣∣f∣∣L​ 不超过 KKK 的条件下，对所有可能满足条件的 fff 取到 Ex∼Pdata[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg[f(x)]\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{data}}[f(x)]-\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{g}}[f(x)]Ex∼Pdata​​[f(x)]−Ex∼Pg​​[f(x)] 的上界，然后再除以 KKK。

    • 把函数 fff 用一个带参数的神经网络来表示

      W(Pdata,Pg)=max⁡w∈WEx∼Pdata[fw(x)]−Ex∼Pg[fw(x)]W(P_{data}, P_{g})=\max _{w \in W} \mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{data}}[f_{w}(x)]-\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{g}}[f_{w}(x)] W(Pdata​,Pg​)=w∈Wmax​Ex∼Pdata​​[fw​(x)]−Ex∼Pg​​[fw​(x)]

    • www 还要满足原来的限制 ∣∣fw∣∣L≤K||f_w||_L\le K∣∣fw​∣∣L​≤K，但我们其实不关心具体的K是多少，它只是会使得梯度变大K倍，并不会影响梯度的方向。此处简化为限制所有的参数 www 不超过某个范围 [−c,c][-c,c][−c,c]，此时关于输入样本 xxx 的导数 ∂fw∂x\frac{\partial f_w}{\partial x}∂x∂fw​​ 也不会超过某个范围，所以一定存在某个不知道的常数 KKK 使得 fwf_wfw​ 的局部变动幅度不会超过它，Lipschitz连续条件得以满足。

    • The loss of discriminator (Remove the last sigmoid layer)

      Ld=Ex∼Pdata[fw(x)]−Ex∼Pg[fw(x)]L_d=\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{data}}[f_{w}(x)]-\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{g}}[f_{w}(x)] Ld​=Ex∼Pdata​​[fw​(x)]−Ex∼Pg​​[fw​(x)]

    • fwf_wfw​'s goal: maximise Wasserstein distance between real data distribution and generative distribution

    • The Loss of generator

      Lg=−Ex∼Pg[fw(x)]=−Ez∼p(z)[fw(gθ(z))]L_g=-\mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{g}}[f_{w}(x)]=-\mathbb{E}_{z \sim p(z)}[f_{w}(g_{\theta}(z))] Lg​=−Ex∼Pg​​[fw​(x)]=−Ez∼p(z)​[fw​(gθ​(z))]

    • gθg_\thetagθ​'s goal: minimise Wasserstein distance between real data distribution and generative distribution

  • Compare to GAN

    • Remove the last sigmoid
    • no log is applied to the loss
    • limit the parameters www within a certain range [−c,c][-c,c][−c,c] (weight clipping)
